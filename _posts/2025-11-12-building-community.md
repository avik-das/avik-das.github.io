It's been a while since I wrote about [Movember](https://mobro.co/akdas), a charity focused on men's health. I take part in the annual fundraising campaign because, while everyone deals with health issues, I can at least speak to my lived experience about how men are affected by such issues. In particular, I can speak to the mental health aspect.

This year, I have been blessed to find community among my peers, and that aided my mental health journey in a way I didn't believe possible. If I can convince you of anything, it's that **you _can_ find community that works for you and doing so worth the effort**.

Good community brightens your daily life, supports you in your hardest times, and above all, enables you to do more than you could alone. But I felt I was locked out of some sort of Premium Life&trade;, for many reasons. I have some close friends I can lean on, friends I would not trade for the world, but I couldn't scale past the one-on-one interactions. Group settings wore me out, maybe because it took real effort to connect with people. I also felt awkward about entering existing social groups, like I was imposing. After college, I had a friend group too, but I assumed for a long time that was enabled by alcohol breaking down social barriers, something I wasn't willing to depend on at this point in my life.

This futility was corroborated by an interaction I had last year with someone close to me at the time. When I expressed my desire to find community, especially one where I can take off my social mask and be my authentic self, I was met with resistance. I was struggling with being part of that person's social circle, so clearly I was asking for too much when I asked for a community that worked for me.

After a long time, this year has been different. I found multiple social groups that broke down my assumption that I couldn't function in a group setting.
